Max Zhaoyi Wang

MDes Ecologies · Harvard GSD · LEED Green Associate

I work at the intersection of building technology and artificial intelligence — designing the physical and data infrastructure that makes adaptive, high-performance buildings possible.

My entry point is material: TPMS-derived ceramic façade systems, parametric fabrication, and physical prototyping. These aren't just design objects — they are sensor platforms, structured to host moisture probes, particulate meters, and mist delivery channels from the moment of casting. The building skin becomes the first layer of a data mesh.

That mesh feeds digital twins, ML pipelines, and ultimately the operational decisions — HVAC setpoints, irrigation schedules, energy load curves — that determine whether a building performs as designed or bleeds energy year after year. This is the gap I occupy: the translation layer between physical form and intelligent building systems, where computational design logic meets AI infrastructure and real estate value.

At Harvard GSD I study Ecologies alongside Real Estate & Sustainability — combining environmental performance research with ESG quantification (Scope 1/2/3, GRESB, TCFD) and real estate financial modelling. I've built prototypes, filed patents, and translated technical findings into investment theses.
